Mental Health and How to Regain the Balance

Recognizing Anxiety

Notice symptoms like excessive worry, restlessness, or irritability. Recognizing anxiety is the first step toward managing it.

Body-focused repetitive behaviors (BFRBs)

FRBs are a group of mental health disorders where you repeat compulsive actions that harm the body. You might do this to cope with stress, anxiety, or boredom. Common BFRBs include: Trichotillomania - hair-pulling Nail-biting and nail-picking. Cheek, lip, or tongue biting. Trichophagia - Hair-eating

Premenstrual dysphoric disorder (PMDD)e

Premenstrual dysphoric disorder (PMDD) is a severe form of PMS PMDD can affect your mental and physical health. This usually starts from 1 to 2 weeks before your period starts to a few days into your period.3 The symptoms can have a negative impact on your quality of life.4 They can affect your work, relationships and social life.5

Get Physical !!!

Exercise releases “feel good” hormones that reduce feelings of stress and anger. It also helps us feel better about our bodies. It can improve our sleep too. If it involves other people, like being part of a team, a class or a group we see regularly, that can also boost our mental health.

Forest Bathing

Its not what you think!! In Japan, some people do “forest bathing”, which may improve their mental health. We have been living around other animals and plants for thousands of years. Some people say nature helps them feel calmer, more hopeful and less alone.

If you are struggling with mental health issues, it is important to seek help. Talk to a trusted friend, family member, or a mental health professional. Remember, you are not alone, and there are people who care and want to help you.
